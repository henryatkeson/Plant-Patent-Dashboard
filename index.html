Scoring methodology

How the sourcing engine scores targets

The engine now uses two separate scores. Acquisition fit ranks which profiles deserve diligence first for the current target range. IP score ranks the depth and activity of the underlying patent/PBR signal.

Acquisition Fit

Default table sort

Rewards focused crop mix, manageable portfolio size, recent activity, private-company signals, source-backed contact paths, and durable protection.

Size Discipline

Penalty / cap

Large strategic platforms, public institutions, and benchmark-scale owners are capped or downranked even when their IP portfolios are very strong.

Verification Burden

Blocker logic

Profiles without a verified company website, contact path, or legal-owner evidence are kept in research until the holder and operating company are clearer.

Affiliation Gate

Confidence cap

Verified and probable breeder-to-program relationships are shown separately from ownership. Unresearched breeder-only names without legal-owner evidence are capped at 50, while unresolved identities are capped at 25.

Web Evidence

Source-backed only

Ownership, leadership, contacts, and company relationships are shown only when an official or otherwise attributable public source is captured.

Trademark Evidence

Registry status matters

Registered or live marks are distinguished from brand use on a company website. Brand use alone does not prove trademark registration or ownership.

Portfolio Scale

Up to 25 points

The IP score gives credit for portfolio depth, with a cap so very large strategics do not overwhelm the list purely because of size.

Protected IP Share

Up to 20 points

Issued US plant patents and granted CPVO PBR observations matter more than application-only signals. Company ownership and expiration curves use record-specific assignee or holder evidence separately.

Jurisdiction Breadth

Up to 12 points

Programs active across multiple jurisdictions get credit because they are more likely to have real commercialization infrastructure.

Recent Velocity

Up to 12 points

Recent filings in the last five years increase the score because they suggest an active breeding pipeline.

Durability

Up to 8 points

Portfolios with active protection remaining score better than portfolios where most rights appear expired or close to expiry.

Crop Attractiveness

Weighted crop signal

Fruit, tree nut, and vegetable crops are weighted more heavily than lower-priority or less relevant crops.

Owner Confidence

Up to 12 points

Confirmed patent assignees score higher than records where the engine only sees a breeder or inventor name.

Relevant Crop Exposure

Up to 10 points

The score rewards records that fall inside the target universe: fruit, tree nuts, and vegetables.

Recency

Up to 7 points

Owners with recent activity get a small boost so stale portfolios do not rank above currently active programs.

How to read the flags

Useful positive signals

Confirmed patent assignee means the record names the party as the legal owner, not only a breeder.

Multi-jurisdiction program means the party appears in several countries or systems.

Focused crop program means the portfolio is concentrated in one crop, which can indicate a specialized platform.

Succession or diligence signals

Individual owner or sole named breeder can point toward succession-driven opportunities.

Patent/PBR cliff means a meaningful share of protection appears to expire in the next five years.

Dormant portfolio means the owner has records but little or no recent filing activity.

Important caveats

CPVO exports often identify breeders more cleanly than legal holders, so some profiles are breeder-led signals rather than confirmed owners. The dashboard uses company rollups where the relationship is clear, but ambiguous people are left visible instead of being forced into a company.

A breeder affiliation is not a legal-owner conclusion. Employment, co-listing, or participation in a breeding program never transfers that breeder's full record history to the company; only record-specific assignee or holder evidence supports ownership attribution.

Portfolio observations are sourcing signals, not a legal title schedule. The owner-scoped figures and cliff charts include only records that specifically name the profile as assignee or holder; CPVO breeder-only rows remain outside those ownership figures until holder data is available.

Record count is not cultivar count. One cultivar can produce multiple applications, grants, renewals, jurisdictions, and holder records; inherited assignments can also make a portfolio appear smaller until ownership changes are imported.

The score should be used to decide where to diligence first. It should be paired with company size, ownership, trademarks, active licenses, customer base, and direct outreach before treating a target as actionable.
